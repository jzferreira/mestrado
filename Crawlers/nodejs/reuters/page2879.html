Emmys 2011: HBO's 'Boardwalk Empire' Sweeps, 'Deadliest Catch' Snags Reality Win

  • Tweet
  • Share this
  • Email
  • Print

By Diane Garrett at TheWrap

Sat Sep 10, 2011 6:44pm EDT

To the surprise of few, HBO’s period  Prohibition drama “Boardwalk Empire” grabbed a gaggle of Creative Arts Emmys for behind-the-scenes work — its seven wins setting up good prospects for more kudos at the main-event Emmys later this month — and Discovery Channel’s “Deadliest Catch,” with four statuettes,  was recognized for the true grit shown by its cameramen and sound editors.

The star power represented by Justin Timberlake, recognized for a guest spot on “Saturday Night Live,” and Gwyneth Paltrow, honored for her guest spot on “Glee,” came solely via quick  video clips of their performances, as the large hall at the Nokia Theater was light on celebrities if strong on relaxed bonhomie.

SNL’s Seth Myers quipped: “Justin wanted to be here, but we said, `No, they want to see the writers.’”

Opening presenters Chuck Lorre and Jon Cryer won laughs by the precision of their avoidance of mentioning departed “Two and a Half Men” star Charlie Sheen by name. Wondering out loud if their own experience might supply and amusing tale as they introduced the casting award, Cryer confessed knowing “Nothing that amuses me,” and Lorre seconded him: "I’m drawing a blank.”

The legendarily lengthy event was marked by only minor glitches, and although free of F-bombs, featured several s-bombs. "Oh, s**t!" said a "Deadliest Catch" sound editor upon grabbing his award, adding, "Well at least I don't have to bleep that out."

"America's Most Wanted" creator John Walsh was honored with the Governors Award and drew strong applause as he reminisced how his wife had convinced him to accept Fox's offer to make a crime-stopping show by invoking their murdered son, and although the show has left Fox after getting credit for some 1,400 captures, noted that he'll host a two-hour Fox special on Oct. 29.

Timberlake won in two categories – for outstanding music and lyrics on “Saturday Night Live,” as well as Outstanding Guest Actor in a Comedy Series for hosting on that show – during the ceremony.

Loretta Devine, meanwhile, won outstanding guest actress in a drama series for her work on “Grey’s Anatomy.”

“Futurama” was awarded the trophy for outstanding animated program, while the "64th Annual Tony Awards" won in the special class category.

The Creative Arts Emmys, which will air on Reelz next week, and are devoted to many technical awards for categories such as editing, directing, makeup stunt coordination and visual effects.

The Primetime Emmys will air on Fox Sept. 18.
